## A17 – Types of Locks

This section presents 10 different types of locks used in residential, commercial, and high-security environments. Each lock type is supported by visual evidence and a reliable source.

---

### 1. Padlock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a padlock (portable lock used for gates, lockers, and bicycles)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Padlock  

**Description:**  
A padlock is a portable lock with a U-shaped shackle that can be opened and closed. It is commonly used for securing gates, lockers, and storage units.

---

### 2. Deadbolt Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a deadbolt lock (commonly used on exterior doors)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_bolt  

**Description:**  
A deadbolt lock provides stronger security than spring-latch locks because it cannot be easily forced open. It is widely used in residential and commercial buildings.

---

### 3. Knob Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a knob lock (commonly used on interior doors)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lock_and_key  

**Description:**  
Knob locks are typically installed on interior doors. The locking mechanism is located inside the knob, making them less secure for external use.

---

### 4. Lever Handle Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a lever handle lock (used in public and commercial buildings)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lever_tumbler_lock  

**Description:**  
Lever handle locks are easy to operate and commonly used in commercial buildings. They are also accessible for people with disabilities.

---

### 5. Mortise Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a mortise lock (installed داخل door pocket)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortise_lock  

**Description:**  
A mortise lock is installed داخل a pocket cut into the door. It is known for its durability and is commonly used in commercial buildings.

---

### 6. Cam Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a cam lock (used in cabinets and drawers)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cam_lock  

**Description:**  
Cam locks are simple locks used in cabinets, mailboxes, and lockers. They operate by rotating a metal cam to secure or release the lock.

---

### 7. Euro Cylinder Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a euro cylinder lock (common in modern doors)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cylinder_lock  

**Description:**  
Euro cylinder locks are commonly used in modern doors and allow easy replacement of the cylinder without changing the entire lock system.

---

### 8. Smart Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a smart lock (digital lock with app or keypad access)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smart_lock  

**Description:**  
Smart locks use electronic methods such as PIN codes, mobile apps, or RFID to control access. They are widely used in smart homes and offices.

---

### 9. Biometric Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a biometric lock (fingerprint or facial recognition lock)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biometrics  

**Description:**  
Biometric locks use unique biological traits such as fingerprints or facial recognition to grant access, offering higher security and convenience.

---

### 10. Chain / Latch Lock
**Evidence:** Screenshot of a chain lock (secondary door security)  
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Door_chain  

**Description:**  
A chain or latch lock is a secondary security device that allows a door to be partially opened while still restricting entry.

---

## Conclusion

Locks remain a fundamental component of physical security. From traditional mechanical locks to modern smart and biometric systems, each type offers different levels of protection and convenience. Understanding these locks is essential for improving both physical and cybersecurity awareness.
