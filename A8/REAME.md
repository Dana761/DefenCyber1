## IoT Device Example: Google TV

I explored cryptography used in Internet of Things (IoT) devices through Google TV, a smart television platform that connects to cloud-based services for streaming content.

As shown in the figure, Google TV operates as an IoT system where the device communicates with external servers over the internet. This communication is secured using Transport Layer Security (TLS), ensuring that data such as video streams and user information is encrypted during transmission.

In addition, encryption algorithms such as AES are used to protect data confidentiality, while authentication mechanisms may involve Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC) to establish secure communication between the device and cloud services.

This demonstrates how modern IoT devices rely on cryptographic techniques to maintain secure and reliable communication in distributed network environments.
