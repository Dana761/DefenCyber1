# A15 – Recent Security Incidents

This section presents five recent cybersecurity incidents from early 2026. Each case highlights real-world cyberattacks across different sectors, including healthcare, aviation, education, media platforms, and telecommunications.

---

## 1. Covenant Health – Ransomware Attack (January 2026)

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of SecurityWeek article  
- Source: https://www.securityweek.com/covenant-health-data-breach-impacts-478000-individuals/

**Description:**  
A ransomware attack by the Qilin group compromised the systems of Covenant Health, affecting approximately 478,000 individuals. Sensitive healthcare data was exposed, causing operational disruptions and privacy risks.

---

## 2. Tulsa International Airport – Data Security Incident (February 2026)

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of official airport notice  
- Source: https://flytulsa.com/news/notice-of-data-security-incident/

**Description:**  
Tulsa International Airport confirmed that an unauthorized third party accessed internal systems and obtained sensitive personal information, including personally identifiable information (PII). The incident highlights vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure systems.

---

## 3. Sapienza University of Rome – Cyberattack (February 2026)

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of TechCrunch article  
- Source: https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/05/one-of-europes-largest-universities-knocked-offline-for-days-after-cyberattack/

**Description:**  
A cyberattack, likely ransomware-related, forced Sapienza University to shut down its IT systems for several days. This disrupted academic services, including email and online platforms, affecting thousands of students and staff.

---

## 4. Substack – Data Breach (February 2026)

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of TechCrunch article  
- Source: https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/05/substack-confirms-data-breach-affecting-email-addresses-and-phone-numbers/

**Description:**  
Substack confirmed a data breach where an unauthorized party accessed user data, including email addresses and phone numbers. Although passwords and financial data were not exposed, the incident increased the risk of phishing and spam attacks.

---

## 5. Odido Telecom – Large-Scale Data Leak (February 2026)

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of Reuters article  
- Source: https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/hacking-group-begins-leaking-customer-data-dutch-telecom-odido-hack-2026-02-26/

**Description:**  
A hacking group began leaking personal data of millions of customers from Dutch telecom provider Odido. The exposed data included names, contact details, and sensitive identity information, posing serious risks of identity theft and fraud.

---

## Key Insights

These incidents demonstrate that cybersecurity threats continue to evolve across multiple industries. Common patterns include:

- The widespread use of ransomware attacks  
- Unauthorized access to sensitive systems and data  
- Large-scale data breaches affecting millions of users  
- Increased risk of phishing, fraud, and identity theft  

Effective cybersecurity measures such as strong access control, data encryption, continuous monitoring, and incident response planning are essential to mitigate these risks.
