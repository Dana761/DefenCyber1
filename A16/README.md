## A16 – Local Security Incidents (Western Australia)

This section presents three real cybersecurity incidents that occurred in Western Australia. Each case is supported by credible sources and demonstrates different types of cyber threats, including ransomware, data breaches, and phishing attacks.

---

### 1. University of Notre Dame Perth – Ransomware Attack

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of ABC News article showing cyber attack at University of Notre Dame  
- Source: https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-02-19/university-of-notre-dame-cyber-attack/104955012  

**Description:**
A ransomware attack targeted the University of Notre Dame in Perth, significantly disrupting its IT systems. Students and staff were unable to access essential services such as enrolment systems, timetables, and internal communication platforms. The university had to rely on manual processes while recovering from the attack.

**Impact:**
- Disruption to academic operations and services  
- Limited access to student systems and course materials  
- Potential exposure of sensitive data  

**Key Insight:**
This incident demonstrates how ransomware attacks can severely impact service availability and highlights the importance of strong cybersecurity infrastructure and incident response planning.

---

### 2. University of Western Australia – Data Breach

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of ABC News article showing UWA data breach  
- Source: https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-08-11/university-of-western-australia-uwa-suffers-major-data-breach/105636074  

**Description:**
The University of Western Australia (UWA) experienced a cybersecurity breach where unauthorized access exposed password information of thousands of staff and students. As a precaution, all affected accounts were locked, and users were required to reset their passwords.

**Impact:**
- Thousands of users locked out of university systems  
- Disruption to academic and administrative activities  
- Increased risk of credential-based attacks  

**Key Insight:**
This incident highlights the importance of secure authentication systems, monitoring, and early detection of unauthorized access to prevent widespread credential compromise.

---

### 3. City of Albany – Phishing Email Incident

**Evidence:**
- Screenshot of Albany Advertiser article showing phishing incident  
- Source: https://www.albanyadvertiser.com.au/news/albany-advertiser/city-of-albany-issues-alert-over-potential-phishing-email-from-staff-asking-for-personal-details-c-18944455  

**Description:**
A phishing email was sent from a compromised City of Albany staff account after a staff member clicked a malicious link. The email was distributed to both internal and external contacts and directed recipients to a fake login page designed to steal credentials.

**Impact:**
- Risk of credential theft for recipients  
- Potential compromise of internal systems  
- Security risk affecting both staff and the public  

**Key Insight:**
This incident highlights the role of human error in cybersecurity breaches and emphasizes the importance of staff training, phishing awareness, and email security controls.

---

## Conclusion

These incidents demonstrate that organizations in Western Australia—including universities and local government—are increasingly targeted by cyber threats. The cases highlight the importance of implementing strong cybersecurity measures such as multi-factor authentication, staff awareness training, proactive monitoring, and effective incident response strategies.
